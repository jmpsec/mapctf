Official CTF Rules

Following actions are prohibited, unless explicitly told otherwise by event Admins.

Rule 1

Cooperation

No cooperation between teams with independent accounts is allowed at any point during the event. Sharing flags, challenge keys, direct solutions, or revealing hints with other teams is considered cheating. Each team must solve challenges independently using only its own members and work.

Rule 2

Attacking Scoreboard

Do not attack the competition platform, scoreboard, authentication system, or any supporting infrastructure. If you discover a bug or vulnerability in the CTF platform itself, report it to the organizers immediately and do not exploit it for advantage.

Rule 3

Sabotage

Absolutely no sabotaging other teams is permitted. Any action intended to disrupt, delay, or interfere with another team’s progress, access, systems, or communication channels is prohibited, even if done indirectly or as a prank.

Rule 4

Bruteforcing

Brute forcing challenge flags or keys against the scoring site is forbidden. Excessive automated guessing, scripted submission floods, or trial-and-error attacks targeting the flag validation endpoint are not allowed and may result in disqualification.

Rule 5

Denial Of Service

Denial-of-Service activity against the CTF platform, challenge services, or related infrastructure is strictly forbidden. This includes traffic flooding, resource exhaustion, intentional crash attempts, and any behavior that degrades availability for other participants.
